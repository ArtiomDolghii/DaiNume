Importance of Graphic Design in the Arts and Marketing Industry

With the invention of Adobe Photoshop and Adobe Illustrator, the capabilities for students and professional employees in the Arts and Marketing industries have provided a multitude of new developments, features, capabilities, and options when it comes to creating new artwork for both the Arts industry and the Marketing industry. Today’s modern necessities to be inventive and to captivate consumers to gain their interest, their attention, and their consumption of the products or services being sold to them require unique ideas and efficient eye catching methods through graphic design artwork. With the invention of Photoshop in 1987, it has created an infinite possibilities for graphic designer and marketing employees to generate unique and efficient artworks in order to entice future customers in purchasing products or services being sold to them. The multitude of features, options, and possibilities being offered with the tools of programs such as Photoshop and Illustrator has been a revolutionary introduction towards graphic design. During the times before programs such as Photoshop were invented, artists and marketing employees had to rely on artwork being created through pen and paper. With the immense capabilities, tools, and features being offered through software such as Photoshop, the traditional way of creating artwork has been considered outdated by many companies. The efficiency of generating unique and eye-catching artwork through graphic design programs has allowed students studying the art and marketing industry to fast track and progress their new found capabilities through the tools and features offered in such programs. Having the advantages of the tools and features offered in programs such as Photoshop and Illustrator will benefit arts students and marketing students to generate more sales by having their art and marketing advertisements stand out much more when comparing to the traditional arts that would be created through pen and paper.

What is graphic design?

Graphic design is the study of conveying information visually. This can be on the internet, it can be through signs, posters, or through any visual methods. For instance, logos for companies are also often created by graphic designers. It is certainly a part of sales and marketing, advertising, etc, but that's not anywhere near all that graphic design encompasses. Graphic design has a lot of theory behind it, especially if one is specializing in UI/UX. You need to be very aware of who you're designing for, who your targeted audience is, how creative the expected standards are of your methods when generating art for the company you work for. Graphic design is visual communication, with an emphasis on problem solving. There is always a message, an audience, and a surrounding context. The job and tasks as designers is to take all of this into account and to achieve those goals as best we can within the abilities and perimeters of the project. The real specialization is in design fundamentals and theory, which is what should be built through educational studies such as university classes, or online certificates. Specialization beyond that simply comes from experience, from working within a specific area of design.

In today’s modern society with all the developments in the art and marketing industry, advertisement, web development, and UI/UX are in demand. Communication, planning, color theory, visual hierarchy, professionalism and creative flexibility, autonomy and implementing directions and accepting feedback are critical to succeed in the field of graphic design, which correlates directly in the marketing/advertisement industry. Proficiency with standard design programs such as Photoshop, Illustrator, and Gimp are basic requirements in order to succeed in developing yourself as a graphic design artist, and additionally to be hired by successful companies where your creative and problem solving skills are greatly desired. If you want to pursue your passion in graphic design, a degree from a university or multiple certificates are an absolute requirement to progress your knowledge, capabilities, and efficiency within the graphic design industry
